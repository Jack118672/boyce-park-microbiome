Boyce Park Focus

Group Alpha studies pond water from Boyce Park, a county park in a region shaped by old coal mining. The core question is whether the water's microbiome matches a typical pond community or carries a signal consistent with mine drainage, runoff, sediment chemistry, or other local environmental pressures.

Going farther

Stretch Work

Use these only after the required analysis works. They turn the project from a list of names into an argument about what the community can do and whether the pattern is real.

Predict function

Use a tool such as PICRUSt to predict what the community may be able to do. Ask whether predicted functions fit pond water or a mine-influenced setting.

Test separation

Do not only eyeball the PCoA. Run PERMANOVA or ANOSIM and report the grouping, statistic, p-value, and plain-English meaning.

Indicator organisms

Name the taxa that most drive the Boyce Park signal. Separate confident genus-level calls from weaker lower-level guesses.

7-minute demo

Presentation Run of Show

  1. Introduce Boyce Park pond water and the mine-history question.
  2. Show who dominates the community and which calls are strongest.
  3. Explain where BLAST and the pipeline agree or disagree.
  4. Show the control check before trusting the profile.
  5. Use the two PCoA views to explain how Alpha differs from rivers and from itself.
  6. End with the biology story and one next experiment.
