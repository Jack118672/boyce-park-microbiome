Opening story

Why This Pond Is Interesting

The setting

Boyce Park is a county park, but the surrounding region has been shaped by old coal mining. That makes the pond more than just a pretty water sample: it is a place where biology may reflect local chemistry and history.

The question

We are asking whether the microbial community looks like a typical freshwater pond, shows signs that could fit mine drainage or runoff, or lands somewhere in between.

The claim we are building

The strongest version of our answer has to come from multiple evidence types agreeing, not from one colorful plot by itself.

How we identified organisms

From DNA Reads To Names

  1. Rank abundant sequences. We start with the feature table so our manual checks focus on the sequences that matter most.
  2. BLAST top sequences. BLAST works like alignment-as-search: it compares our unknown sequence to reference 16S sequences.
  3. Compare to the pipeline. We place our BLAST calls beside the provided QIIME2/SILVA taxonomy and look for agreement.
  4. Check controls. Blank and distilled-water samples help us avoid treating contamination as a real pond signal.

PCoA evidence

Boyce Park Compared With Rivers

Main point:

PCoA from the shared water feature table places Boyce Park samples mostly on the right side of Axis 1, while the river baseline samples sit mostly to the left.

Proof to point at:

Average Boyce-to-river Bray-Curtis distance is 0.974, higher than the river-within average of 0.609. That supports the claim that Boyce Park does not match the river baseline.

PCoA evidence

Patterns Inside Alpha Samples

Main point:

Within Boyce Park, samples separate by pond sub-location, especially BP5 on the lower-left and BP4 on the right.

Proof to point at:

The proof is repeated samples from the same Boyce sub-location landing near each other. That supports a real within-pond pattern instead of random scatter.

Biology interpretation

Turning Names Into A Story

Claim What type of community does Boyce Park pond water appear to have?
Evidence Use trusted taxa, controls, composition, and both PCoA comparisons.
Reasoning Connect organisms to freshwater ecology, oxygen, nutrients, sediment, runoff, or mine-drainage chemistry.

Going farther

How We Would Strengthen The Argument

Predict function

PICRUSt could estimate what the community may be able to do, then we could ask whether those predicted functions fit a pond or mine-influenced environment.

Test separation

PERMANOVA or ANOSIM would let us test whether a PCoA separation is statistically meaningful instead of just visually interesting.

Find indicator organisms

We could highlight the taxa most responsible for the Boyce Park signal and explain their likely ecological roles.
